Lesson 16: Toy GAN
==================

Goal
----
Learn the basic adversarial training idea behind GANs without starting from full image generation.

This lesson is intentionally tiny.
The "real data" is just a 1D number distribution.

That is enough to teach:
- generator
- discriminator
- alternating updates
- `detach()`
- why GANs can be unstable

What a GAN is
-------------
GAN stands for Generative Adversarial Network.

It contains two models:

Generator:
- starts from random noise
- tries to create fake samples that look real

Discriminator:
- receives real and fake samples
- tries to tell them apart

This creates a game:
- discriminator tries not to be fooled
- generator tries to fool it

Why this lesson uses 1D data
----------------------------
Image GANs are much harder.
They involve:
- large networks
- fragile training
- many failure modes

For a beginner, that can hide the main idea.

Here the real data is just:

    N(mean=2.0, std about 0.7)

So if the generator learns well, its fake outputs should move toward that region.

Generator pipeline
------------------

    random noise z shape (batch, 3)
      -> MLP
      -> fake sample shape (batch, 1)

Discriminator pipeline
----------------------

    sample shape (batch, 1)
      -> MLP
      -> logit shape (batch, 1)

The discriminator outputs one logit:
- large positive = looks real
- large negative = looks fake

Discriminator training step
---------------------------
The discriminator sees:
- real samples labeled 1
- fake samples labeled 0

Loss idea:

    classify real as real
    classify fake as fake

Important line:

    fake_samples = generator(...).detach()

Why detach here?
----------------
Because during discriminator training, we only want to update discriminator parameters.

We do not want gradients from the discriminator loss to flow back into the generator
during this step.

`detach()` means:

    use these fake values
    but cut them off from the generator graph

Generator training step
-----------------------
Then we do a second step:

    generated = generator(...)
    fooled_logits = discriminator(generated)

Now the generator wants the discriminator to predict:

    real (1)

for the fake samples.

So generator loss means:

    make fake samples that fool the discriminator

Why there are two optimizers
----------------------------
The two models have different parameters and different objectives.

So we use:
- one optimizer for the generator
- one optimizer for the discriminator

Alternating updates
-------------------
The loop is:

    1. update discriminator
    2. update generator

This alternating structure is one of the defining ideas of GAN training.

Why GANs are hard
-----------------
Unlike ordinary classifiers, the target is moving.

Why?
Because:
- the discriminator changes
- the generator changes
- each one responds to the other

So the optimization landscape keeps shifting.

Common GAN failure modes
------------------------
Even though this tiny example is simpler, real GANs can suffer from:
- instability
- one network overpowering the other
- mode collapse

Mode collapse means the generator produces limited varieties of outputs.

What `fake_mean` tells you
--------------------------
The script prints:

    fake_mean

Since the real data is centered near 2.0, this gives a rough quick check:

    is the generator moving toward the real distribution?

It is not a full evaluation, but it is a useful simple signal.

Relationship to earlier lessons
-------------------------------
GANs reuse ideas you already learned:
- linear layers
- logits
- BCEWithLogits-style thinking
- `detach()`
- optimizer steps

The new part is that now two networks interact.

Common beginner mistakes
------------------------
1. Forgetting why fake samples are detached in the discriminator step
2. Thinking generator and discriminator are trained with the same goal
3. Forgetting that generator wants discriminator to output "real" for fake samples
4. Expecting GAN losses to behave as smoothly as normal classifier losses
5. Starting with image GANs before understanding the training game

Exercises
---------
1. Change the real-data mean from 2.0 to -1.0 and observe `fake_mean`.
2. Increase generator noise dimension from 3 to 8.
3. Increase hidden width from 16 to 32.
4. Print a few generated samples every 50 steps.

Self-check questions
--------------------
1. What is the job of the generator?
2. What is the job of the discriminator?
3. Why do we detach fake samples during the discriminator step?
4. Why are there two optimizers?
5. Why are GANs usually harder to train than ordinary classifiers?
